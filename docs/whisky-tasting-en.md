# Whisky Tasting Notes — Knowledge Base

## 1. The Tasting Process

Whisky tasting follows five structured steps.

### Step 1: Appearance / Colour

Tilt the glass at 45 degrees against a white background and observe the colour.

| Colour | Indication |
|---|---|
| Pale Gold | Young maturation, ex-bourbon cask |
| Amber | Medium maturation |
| Copper / Mahogany | Sherry cask influence, long maturation |
| Dark Brown | Very long maturation or heavily sherried cask |

**Legs:** The speed and volume of droplets running down the inside of the glass indicate viscosity and oiliness.

### Step 2: Nose / Aroma

Approximately 80% of perceived flavour comes from the sense of smell.

1. Slowly bring the glass to your nose, breathing in through the mouth for the first impression
2. Gradually insert your nose closer to the rim to detect more complex aromas
3. Add a few drops of water and nose again — this can open up new aromas

### Step 3: Palate / Taste

Take a small sip and spread it across the entire tongue, holding for 15–30 seconds. Key elements to assess:

- **Mouthfeel:** Viscosity, texture, smoothness
- **Attack:** Initial intensity upon entry
- **Development:** How flavours evolve and deepen
- **Balance:** Harmony of sweetness, spice, acidity, and bitterness

### Step 4: Finish / Aftertaste

The length and quality of flavour that lingers after swallowing.

| Length | Duration |
|---|---|
| Short | Under 15 seconds |
| Medium | 15–45 seconds |
| Long | 45 seconds – 2 minutes |
| Very Long / Endless | Over 2 minutes |

### Step 5: Adding Water (Optional)

A few drops of room-temperature water break down ethyl esters, releasing different flavour compounds. The optimal drinking strength is generally considered to be 20–30% ABV.

## 2. The Flavour Wheel (SWRI Pentland Flavour Wheel)

The industry-standard flavour wheel developed by the Scotch Whisky Research Institute (SWRI) in the 1970s. It comprises 8 major categories: categories 1–6 derive from fermentation and distillation, while 7–8 derive from maturation.

### Category 1: Cereal

| Subcategory | Examples |
|---|---|
| Cooked Mash | Porridge, rice pudding |
| Cooked Veg | Boiled cabbage, corn |
| Husky | Straw, dry grain |
| Malt Extract | Biscuit, malt sugar |
| Yeasty | Bread, brewer's yeast |

### Category 2: Fruity

| Subcategory | Examples |
|---|---|
| Citric | Orange, lemon, grapefruit |
| Fresh Fruit | Apple, pear, peach, apricot |
| Cooked Fruit | Stewed apple, marmalade, jam |
| Dried Fruit | Raisin, fig, prune, fruit cake |
| Solvent | Nail polish remover (excessive esters) |

### Category 3: Floral

| Subcategory | Examples |
|---|---|
| Fragrant | Lavender, rose water, perfume |
| Greenhouse | Geranium, fresh flowers |
| Leafy | Fresh-cut grass, pine nuts |
| Hay | Dried hay, heather, herbs, sage |

### Category 4: Peaty

| Subcategory | Examples |
|---|---|
| Medicinal | Iodine, antiseptic, plaster |
| Smoky | Campfire, smoked wood, coal tar |
| Kippery | Smoked herring, smoked salmon |
| Mossy | Wetland, peat bog, earthy |

### Category 5: Feinty

| Subcategory | Examples |
|---|---|
| Honey | Honey, golden syrup, beeswax |
| Leathery | Old leather, tanned hide |
| Tobacco | Cigar, cigarette |
| Sweat & Plastic | Sweat, rubber |

### Category 6: Sulphury

| Subcategory | Examples |
|---|---|
| Coal Gas | Struck match, sulphur |
| Rubbery | Tyre, rubber |
| Vegetative | Cabbage, onion (sulphur compounds) |

### Category 7: Woody *(maturation-derived)*

| Subcategory | Examples |
|---|---|
| Toasted | Toast, coffee grounds, liquorice |
| Vanilla / Custard | Custard, sponge cake, toffee |
| Old Wood | Mould, cardboard, pencil shavings, ink |
| New Wood | Resin, cigar box, ginger, black pepper |

### Category 8: Winey *(maturation-derived)*

| Subcategory | Examples |
|---|---|
| Sherried | Wine, port, Madeira |
| Nutty | Walnut, hazelnut, almond |
| Chocolate | Milk chocolate, cacao, dark chocolate |
| Oily | Candle wax, olive oil |

## 5. Regional Characteristics

### Scotch Whisky — 5 Protected Regions

| Region | Character | Key Flavours |
|---|---|---|
| **Speyside** | Fruity, elegant, sweet | Apple, pear, honey, sherry, chocolate |
| **Highland** | Diverse, medium-bodied | Heather, honey, fruit, occasionally smoky |
| **Islay** | Heavy peat, iodine, coastal | Smoke, iodine, seaweed, smoked fish, salt |
| **Lowland** | Light, soft, triple-distilled | Grass, grain, floral, creamy |
| **Campbeltown** | Briny, oily, coastal | Sea salt, toffee, vanilla, light smoke |
| **Islands** | Between Speyside and Islay | Sea salt, heather, spice, mild smoke |

### Bourbon

- **Legal requirements:** New charred American white oak casks only; minimum 51% corn in the mash bill
- **Key flavours:** Vanilla, caramel, butterscotch, corn, charred oak
- **Sub-styles:** Rye whiskey (spicy), wheat whiskey (soft)

### Irish Whiskey

- **Character:** Smoothness from triple distillation; use of unmalted barley
- **Key flavours:** Honey, vanilla, light fruit, creamy

### Japanese Whisky

- **Character:** Rooted in Scotch traditions; emphasises delicacy and harmony (*wa*)
- **Key flavours:** Delicate fruit, floral, light smoke
- **Mizunara cask:** Sandalwood, incense, vanilla, tonka bean

### Other Regions

| Region | Character |
|---|---|
| Canadian | Light, spicy (rye-driven), mostly blended |
| Indian | Fast maturation in tropical climate, spicy |
| Tasmanian | Fruity, clean, characterful |

## 6. ABV & the Effect of Adding Water

| ABV | Character |
|---|---|
| 40% | EU legal minimum (Scotch); flavours are mild |
| 43–46% | Well-balanced; aromas open more freely |
| 46%+ | Recommended threshold for non-chill filtration; oily compounds retained |
| 52–66% | Cask strength; all flavour compounds preserved in full |

### Effect of Adding Water

- **Chemical effect:** Breaks down ethyl esters, releasing previously masked flavour compounds
- **Recommended amount:** Start with 2–3 drops and adjust to taste (optimal strength: 20–30% ABV)
- **Caution:** Ice or cold water suppresses aromas and is not recommended for tasting

## 7. Glassware

| Glass | Character | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Glencairn** | Tulip-shaped, heavy base, wide bowl tapering to a narrow rim | Standard tasting glass; concentrates aromas effectively |
| **Copita** | Tall stem, small aperture (sherry glass-derived) | High-strength or delicate whiskies; stem prevents hand warming |
| **Tulip glass** | Similar to copita | Professional blind tasting |
| **Tumbler / Rock glass** | Wide, heavy base | Casual drinking (not recommended for tasting) |

## 8. Professional Scoring Systems

### Jim Murray's Whisky Bible (most widely referenced)

100-point scale across 4 categories of 25 points each.

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Nose (25 pts) | Aromas detected through multiple nosings |
| Taste (25 pts) | How flavours present and develop on the palate |
| Finish (25 pts) | The quality and character of the aftertaste |
| Balance (25 pts) | Overall complexity and harmony |

**Score guide:**

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 80+ | Worth trying |
| 85+ | Very good |
| 90+ | Exceptional |
| 95+ | Liquid gold; a world classic |

### Other Notable Reviewers & Publications

| Reviewer / Publication | Notes |
|---|---|
| Whisky Advocate | 100-point scale; multiple blind tasters by category |
| Whisky Magazine / World Whiskies Awards | Gold, silver, bronze awards by category |
| WhiskyFun (Serge Valentin) | Independent blogger; 100-point scale; extensive database |

## 9. Features for a Tasting Note App

### Core Features

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| Tasting note entry | Structured recording of colour, nose, palate, finish, and overall score |
| Rating system | 100-point scale or 5-star format |
| Bottle database | Distillery, region, vintage information |
| Collection management | Owned bottles list with quantity and fill-level tracking |
| Wishlist | Bottles the user wants to try or acquire |

### Important Features

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| Flavour wheel / flavour tags | Visual UI for selecting flavour descriptors |
| Flavour chart | Radar chart showing flavour profile balance |
| Regional filtering | Filter by Scotch / Bourbon / Japanese / etc. |
| Community features | Browse other users' reviews |

### Differentiating Features

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| Tasting flight management | Record and compare multiple bottles in a single session |
| Photo attachment | Attach photos of the bottle or glass to notes |
| Blind tasting mode | Hide bottle identity for unbiased practice |
| Data export | Export notes as CSV |

### Key UI/UX Considerations

- **Guided flow for beginners:** Step-by-step input that prompts what to look for at each stage
- **Tap-to-select flavour wheel:** Visual descriptor selection instead of free text entry
- **Offline support:** Essential for use at a cellar, bar, or whisky festival (core PWA requirement)
- **Simple mode vs. detailed mode:** Switchable depth for casual vs. enthusiast use

## 10. Key Tasting Terminology Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Nose / Aroma | The aromas perceived by smelling the whisky |
| Palate / Taste | The flavours perceived in the mouth |
| Finish / Aftertaste | Lingering flavours and sensations after swallowing |
| Complexity | Multiple layered flavours that evolve over time |
| Body | The weight and richness of the whisky in the mouth |
| Mouthfeel | The texture and physical sensation in the mouth |
| Peaty | Smoky character derived from peat-dried malt |
| Phenolic | Phenol compound character — medicinal, iodine-like |
| Cask Strength | Bottled directly from the cask without dilution |
| Non-Chill Filtered (NCF) | Bottled without cold filtration; retains oily compounds |
| Single Malt | Produced at a single distillery from malted barley only |
| Blended | A blend of malt and grain whiskies from multiple distilleries |
| Age Statement | The number of years the youngest whisky in the bottle was matured |
| NAS | No Age Statement — maturation age not disclosed |
| Nosing | The act of smelling the whisky |
| Dram | A measure of whisky (informal term for a pour) |
| Vatting | Combining multiple casks of the same type |
| Expression | A specific bottling or variant from a distillery |
